About boom hunter (discord)

Boom hunter tracks price action through its price channel. The longer the lengths and more smoothing you use the bigger the channel. Also the slower the timeframe you use the bigger the channel. Price action bounces in its channel until it breaks it, when it breaks the channel this is known as a dump or a pump. In theory there is only 2 types of trades you can make: A trade within the channel or a trade that leaves the channel.

The boom hunter oscillator tracks SR levels and only moves when levels are getting broken. This makes it ideal for finding patterns like double bottoms and head and shoulders. It also makes it the perfect tool to find/setup breakouts.

Channels

A parallel channel has a resistance band at the top and a support band on the bottom. The median pushes against these. The pressure from SR is similar to a magnetic pull, boom hunter uses a filter that emulates this effect.

Price action will bounce between these bands until it breaks out from its trend line

When channels break they will typically either halve or double

The big moves happen when the channels get broken

On boom hunter we can track these bands horizontally or using Signals XL we can do it dynamically

SR and channels

The SR bands on Signals XL show you which band is getting tested

When the oscillator pushes/breaks the resistance band it shows price action attempting to break the channel. A pullback below is a confirmation of rejection from the band, at this point price action will travel in the direction of the support band.

The bars on the SR bands show the strength of the SR. These get stronger as SR gets stronger. At first price action will get pulled to the active SR band before getting pushed away

Crossovers above the Support band with bars can be considered as potential long entries

If you enter the crossover after channel resistance is broken you are entering a pump

When the SR strength bars stop inside the SR bands we have a signal for a continuation of trend

The light colored bars show SR strength is peaking. This means price action is pushing/testing a SR band. Price action can either pass through or get rejected. Either way it will be released from the pressure and go for a fly. 

Trend and channels

In a down trend channel the strong trades are shorts from the top. In an up trend channel the strong trades are longs from the bottom. In a sideways channel it is both.

A down trend channel is considered bullish because at some time it will break to make an up trend channel.

In most cases price action will make a pivot above the channel support before it attempts to break the resistance
