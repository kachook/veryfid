  • Good for finding entries, identifying pivots and pump and dump

  • Helps in determining the momentum and trend

  • Choice of many types of Moving Averages
  • Works on all time frames
  • Loaded with all types of Alerts

Entries

Using Oscillator 1, you can find many types of pullbacks.

Strategy 1: With this one, if it is in up trend (green) and pullback into red and straight back to green without crossing under, it is usually a good movement and entry.

Strategy 2:  You can look for entries after the indicator changes to red or green.

Close up

Live analysis of trend following 

First it breaks it trend line so you know it will want to come back to test, this is the first pullback. It then makes a double bottom (boom). You can see on COGXL osc1 that it does not break into the red. Then it makes another pullback. This time a harder one that dips in the red on osc1. It then makes a boom pattern double bottom on BHXL. At this point it makes a new pivot and trend line. You can then catch it coming down

Pressure

Example from a live analysis: 4hr shows it has gone into green; this is a setup for both directions. If the oscillator can't get back into the green and instead crosses under the ZLSMA, we have a solid short.

If it can get back into the green, there will be a nice long.

In another scenario, predictions can go like this, following the moving average as the trend.

Automatic Length can be activated for a smoother and sharper representation of the pressure in red or green shadow. It will work well on the higher timeframes.
